Sinigang

Sinigang is a Filipino sour soup or stew characterized by its tangy flavor, usually achieved using tamarind as the souring agent. It typically includes meat (often pork or shrimp), vegetables, and sometimes seafood.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork belly or pork ribs, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 10-12 pieces string beans, cut into 2-inch lengths
  • 2-3 pieces green chili peppers
  • 1 packet (about 40g) sinigang mix (tamarind soup base)
  • Salt and fish sauce (patis) to taste

Method

  1. In a large pot, bring about 2 liters of water to a boil.
  2. Add the pork pieces and simmer for about 30 minutes or until pork is tender.
  3. Once the pork is tender, add the onions, tomatoes, and radish to the pot. Simmer for about 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in the sinigang mix until dissolved.
  5. Add the eggplant, string beans, and green chili peppers. Simmer for another 5-7 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  6. Season with salt and fish sauce to taste.
  7. Finally, add the kangkong (water spinach) and simmer for another 2 minutes.
  8. Remove from heat and serve hot with steamed rice.
